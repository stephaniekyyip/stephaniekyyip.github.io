Automated Dancing Puppet

Third year electrical and computer engineering lab project

Overview

As a part of my electrical engineering labs during junior year, I worked with a lab partner to implement a marionette puppet that performed a dance routine on command. The idea behind the project was to become familiar with lab equipment and to develop engineering troubleshooting and teamwork skills by working towards a project throughout the school year.

How the Puppet Works

The puppet’s movements were controlled by various circuits and programmed in C using a Raspberry Pi. A motor and a connected optical encoder allowed for the control of the puppet’s limbs, which included moving each limb up or down by a designated distance.

My Role in the Project

Though the lab instructors had done the final design for both the hardware and software, my lab partner and I were responsible for the construction, testing, and troubleshooting of the puppet. We had to read circuit schematics, build and test the circuits and hardware, modify C code to control the puppet’s dance moves, and integrate and troubleshoot both the software and hardware. I focused mainly on the software side of the project amd worked together with my partner on other areas of the project, particularly the troubleshooting and testing aspects.
